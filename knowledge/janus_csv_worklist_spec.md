# JANUS CSV Worklist Specification

## Scope

This specification describes the synthetic JANUS-style CSV artifacts generated by LabFlow normalization workflows. It is for dry-run previews, audit explanations, and portfolio demos only.

## Synthetic And Non-Production Note

This document is synthetic. It is not a robot vendor SOP, production lab instruction, clinical workflow, or proprietary automation procedure. CSV examples are not robot-ready instructions for real execution.

## Artifact Types

LabFlow models two CSV-like artifact views:

- Minimal worklist preview: destination well, diluent volume, and sample volume.
- Audit worklist preview: batch ID, sample ID, source/destination location, status, normalization mode, and transfer volumes.

The minimal preview exists to show the robot-facing shape. The audit preview exists to support traceability and review.

## Gating Rules

- JANUS-style artifacts require deterministic validation.
- Invalid batches are blocked.
- Invalid batches cannot generate JANUS worklists.
- Invalid samples must not appear in robot transfer rows.
- Dry-run preview is the safe default.
- State-changing commit actions require a prior dry-run event ID, an explicit approval token, deterministic validation pass, and audit event.
- Until a durable approval and artifact store exists, commit-mode generation must remain blocked.
- The agent must not fabricate sample IDs, concentrations, destinations, standards, blanks, or worklist rows.

## Human SOP Alignment

Human SOPs and controlled records usually separate reviewed calculations from operator-facing artifacts. LabFlow adapts that pattern by requiring deterministic validation before any JANUS-style dry-run preview. The CSV shape is synthetic and portfolio-safe; it is not a vendor robot SOP, not production instructions, and not a substitute for lab approval records.

## Ordering And Formatting Rules

- Rows follow deterministic A1-H12 plate order where plate location is relevant.
- Volumes are represented in `uL`.
- Volumes must come from deterministic normalization planning, not LLM arithmetic.
- A blocked response should include errors and audit metadata; it should not silently write files.

## Diagnostic And Exception Codes

- `JANUS_BLOCKED_FOR_INVALID_BATCH`: worklist generation requested with unresolved errors.
- `COMMIT_MODE_NOT_AVAILABLE`: commit generation is blocked until durable approval controls exist.
- `MISSING_CONCENTRATION`: concentration required to plan a transfer is absent.
- `SAMPLE_TRANSFER_BELOW_MINIMUM`: transfer cannot be represented as standard normalization.
- `DESTINATION_VOLUME_EXCEEDED`: destination would exceed working volume.
- `REQUIRED_ARTIFACT_MISSING`: required plan, curve, or manifest is absent.
